About Soil

Soil is the upper layer of the earth where plants grow. It is made up of minerals, organic matter, water, and air. Healthy soil is essential for agriculture, ecosystems, and life itself.

Types of Soil

  • Sandy Soil: Drains quickly but has low fertility.
  • Clay Soil: Rich in nutrients but heavy and slow-draining.
  • Loamy Soil: A balanced mix of sand, silt, and clay; ideal for farming.
  • Silty Soil: Smooth texture, retains moisture well.

Importance of Soil

Soil provides plants with essential nutrients, supports biodiversity, and helps regulate water. It also plays a vital role in carbon storage, making it crucial for combating climate change.

How to Care for Soil

  1. Avoid overuse of chemical fertilizers.
  2. Practice crop rotation to maintain fertility.
  3. Add organic matter like compost or manure.
  4. Prevent erosion by planting cover crops.

Protecting soil means protecting life. Healthy soil ensures food security and a sustainable environment for future generations.
